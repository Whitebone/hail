How clouds form

Clouds form with ice crystals and water droplets attaching to CCN. CCN stands for cloud condesnation nuclei. The water gets to the CCN from the sun hitting the ground, and warming it up through radiation, but also warms up water, and makes it evaporate. From the ground being warm, through conduction it makes the evaporated water warmer, but, since warmer particles are less dense, they rise which allows the water to get to the CCN. Ice crystals form since higher altitudes equal lower temperature, so if the updrafts get high enough up, it can become an ice crystal.

How Hail forms

Hail forms when the cloud has formed, and the updrafts have brought the cloud high enough so water can condense and freeze. From here, hail forms and grows in convection currents. Once the hail forms, and grows, it will eventually get too heavy or updrafts to support it, so the hail will then start to fall.

Summary

Hail is able to fall, once clouds form with warm air and water vapor rising high enough for it to stick on to CCN and condense or freeze, then from there ice crystals and water droplets stick together until its too heavy for the updrafts to support, which makes it fall.
